## Enroute Charts
- Airways below 18,000 feet MSL are depicted on Low Altitude Enroute Charts and are called **Victor Airways.**
- Airways at or above 18,000 feet MSL and up to FL450 are shown on High Altitude Enroute Charts and are called **Jet Routes.**

## Terminology

| Term                                     | Description & Requirements                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
|:---------------------------------------- |:----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| ⭐️MEA                                   | - *obstruction; signal*<br> - **Minimum Enroute Altitude** generally guarantees both **obstruction clearance** and **navigation signal coverage**, and is normally the lowest altitude you use on an airway.                                                                                                                                                                              |
| ⭐️MOCA                                  | - *sp condition; 22nm VOR; not guaranteed*<br> - **Minimum Obstruction Clearance Altitude** may be **assigned by ATC** when certain special conditions exist, and when within **22 nm** of a VOR. A MOCA does not guarantee you will receive a reliable navigation signal if you're more than 22 nm from the facility. **Identifiable by the letter T.**                                  |
| ⭐️MRA                                   | - *navaid; intersection*<br> - **Minimum Authorized Altitude** is designated where a minimum altitude is needed to **receive a navaid** away from the airway being flown in order to identify an **intersection**.                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ⭐️MCA                                   | - *low2high; climb prior*<br> - **Minimum Crossing Altitude** is the lowest altitude which an aircraft can cross when proceeding in direction of higher minimum enroute altitude (MEA). You must **begin climbing prior to reaching the fix** in order to arrive over the fix at the MCA.                                                                                                 |
| ⭐️MAA                                   | - *line-of-sight; VOR*<br> - **Maximum Authorized Altitude** is the highest flight altitude based on the **line-of-sight** transmitting distance of VOR stations using the same VOR frequency. It guarantees receiving only one signal at a time on a given frequency.                                                                                                                    |
| ⭐️Grid MORA (OROCA)                     | - *Jeppesen; 1k; m2k*<br> - **Grid Minimum Off Route Altitude** is an altitude derived by Jeppesen that provides terrain and man-made structure clearance within the grid outline by latitude and longitude lines. **In non-mountain areas, the clearance is 1,000 ft. In mountainous areas, the clearance is 2,000 ft.**                                                                 |
| Minimum Sector Altitude (MSA)            | - *Jeppeson; 1k25*<br> - The minimum safe/sector altitude (MSA), shown on Jeppesen approach charts, provides **1,000 feet of obstruction clearance within 25 nautical miles** of the indicated facility, unless some other distance is specified.                                                                                                                                         |
| Straight in Approach                     | An approach executed without performing the published course reversal.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| Course Reversal                          | - *180*<br> - Course reversal is the course to **turn the aircraft around by 180 degrees** therefore lining the aircraft up with the runway.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| Straight in Landing                      | - *30*<br> - Straight in landing minimums normally are used when final approach course is positioned within 30 degree of the runway, and a minimum of maneuvering is required to align the airplane with the runway.                                                                                                                                                                      |
| Circle to Landing                        | If final approach is not properly aligned, or if it is desirable to landing on a different runway, circling approach may be executed and circling approach minimums apply.                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Touchdown Zone Elevation (TDZE)          | - *3000*<br> - The highest point in the first 3,000 feet of the runway landing surface from MSL.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Airport/field Elevation                  | - *usable; ft*<br> - The highest point of an airport's usable runways measured in feet from MSL.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Threshold Crossing Height (TCH)          | - *AGL; continue*<br> - The **AGL** height at which you cross the threshold if you continue the approach to a landing while remaining on the glide slope/path.                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| Standard Instrument Departure (SID)      | - A standard instrument departure (SID) is depicted in graphic form and is identified by an abbreviated name and numeral, then a dot followed by the name of the exit or transition fix.<br> - If you do not have the chart for the procedure with you, do not accept a SID clearance. If you do not want to use a SID, indicate "**NO SID**" in the remarks section of your flight plan. |
| Departure End of Runway (DER)            | The physical end of the runway opposite the landing threshold used for an aircraft's takeoff.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| Air Route Traffic Control Center (ARTCC) | A facility established to provide air traffic control service to aircraft operating on instrument flight rules (IFR) flight plans within controlled airspace.                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
