## ⭐️ Enroute
- Airways below 18,000 feet MSL are depicted on Low Altitude Enroute Charts and are called **Victor Airways.**
- Airways at or above 18,000 feet MSL and up to FL450 are shown on High Altitude Enroute Charts and are called **Jet Routes.**
- Victor Airways - Airway extends **more than 51 nm from each navaid** includes the airspace between lines **diverging at angles of 4.5 degrees** from the center of each navaid.
- If use **GPS** for navigation, you might be using **RNAV routes.**
	- Tango Routes (with letter T) are established around or through busy terminal areas.
	- Waypoints (star symbol)
	- **MEA might be lower for GPS/WAAS, designated with letter G**
- Airports
	- Military airports are round
	- **Airports in BLUE have instrument approach**; brown(FAA)/Green(Jepp) have no instrument approach.
- ==KEY TERMINOLOGY==: MEA, MOCA, MRA, MCA, MAA, MORA

## ⭐️Approach
### Performing Approach
| Type                                     | Description / Condition                                                                                                                   |
|:---------------------------------------- |:----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Straight-In ==Approach== (NoPT)**          | Published course reversal must not be performed. Used with DME Arc, Radar Vectors, fixes aligned with final course, or NoPT sectors.      |
| **Course Reversal**                      | Turns the aircraft around 180 degrees to align with the runway and allows descent to a reasonable final approach altitude.                | 
| **Straight-In ==Landing==**                  | Used when the final approach course is aligned within 30° of the runway and minimal maneuvering is required to land.                      |
| **Circling-to-Land (Circling Approach)** | Executed when the approach is not properly aligned with the runway or landing on a different runway is required; circling minimums apply. |

## Terminology
| Term                                     | Description & Requirements                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| :--------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| MEA                                    | - *obstruction; signal*<br> - **Minimum Enroute Altitude** generally guarantees both **obstruction clearance** and **navigation signal coverage**, and is normally the lowest altitude you use on an airway.                                                                                                                                                                              |
| MOCA                                   | - *sp condition; 22nm VOR; not guaranteed*<br> - **Minimum Obstruction Clearance Altitude** may be **assigned by ATC** when certain special conditions exist, and when within **22 nm** of a VOR. A MOCA does not guarantee you will receive a reliable navigation signal if you're more than 22 nm from the facility. **Identifiable by the letter T.**                                  |
| MRA                                    | - *navaid; intersection*<br> - **Minimum Authorized Altitude** is designated where a minimum altitude is needed to **receive a navaid** away from the airway being flown in order to identify an **intersection**.                                                                                                                                                                        |
| MCA                                    | - *low2high; climb prior*<br> - **Minimum Crossing Altitude** is the lowest altitude which an aircraft can cross when proceeding in direction of higher minimum enroute altitude (MEA). You must **begin climbing prior to reaching the fix** in order to arrive over the fix at the MCA.                                                                                                 |
| MAA                                    | - *line-of-sight; VOR*<br> - **Maximum Authorized Altitude** is the highest flight altitude based on the **line-of-sight** transmitting distance of VOR stations using the same VOR frequency. It guarantees receiving only one signal at a time on a given frequency.                                                                                                                    |
| Grid MORA (OROCA)                      | - *Jeppesen; 1k; m2k*<br> - **Grid Minimum Off Route Altitude** is an altitude derived by Jeppesen that provides terrain and man-made structure clearance within the grid outline by latitude and longitude lines. **In non-mountain areas, the clearance is 1,000 ft. In mountainous areas, the clearance is 2,000 ft.**                                                                 |
| Minimum Sector Altitude (MSA)            | - *Jeppeson; 1k25*<br> - The minimum safe/sector altitude (MSA), shown on Jeppesen approach charts, provides **1,000 feet of obstruction clearance within 25 nautical miles** of the indicated facility, unless some other distance is specified.                                                                                                                                         |
| Touchdown Zone Elevation (TDZE)          | - *3000*<br> - The highest point in the first 3,000 feet of the runway landing surface from MSL.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Airport/field Elevation                  | - *usable; ft*<br> - The highest point of an airport's usable runways measured in feet from MSL.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Threshold Crossing Height (TCH)          | - *AGL; continue*<br> - The **AGL** height at which you cross the threshold if you continue the approach to a landing while remaining on the glide slope/path.                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| Standard Instrument Departure (SID)      | - A standard instrument departure (SID) is depicted in graphic form and is identified by an abbreviated name and numeral, then a dot followed by the name of the exit or transition fix.<br> - If you do not have the chart for the procedure with you, do not accept a SID clearance. If you do not want to use a SID, indicate "**NO SID**" in the remarks section of your flight plan. |
| Departure End of Runway (DER)            | The physical end of the runway opposite the landing threshold used for an aircraft's takeoff.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| Air Route Traffic Control Center (ARTCC) | A facility established to provide air traffic control service to aircraft operating on instrument flight rules (IFR) flight plans within controlled airspace.                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| Terminal Instrument Procedures (TERPs)  | Standards for a specific clearance from obstacle at a given distance from the runway based on an aircraft’s climbing at least **200 feet per nautical mile.**                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
