## ⭐️ Departure
### General IFR Departure Options 
1. SID
2. ODP
3. Vectors: ATC provides vectors to direct out of the terminal area until intersection.
4. VFR Departure: When conditions permit, depart under VFR and **obtain clearance from ARTCC**
### Departure Charts (DP)
#### 1. Standard Instrument Departures (SIDs)
- depicted in graphic form
- **Must receive an ATC clearance to fly a SID.**
- Indicate "NO SID" in remarks section of your flight plan if not flying a SID.

##### 1.1 Vector SID 
- **ATC** provides radar vectors that start just after takeoff until you reach your assigned route.
- Jeppeson chart provides additional terrain information.
- ==KEY TERMINOLOGY==: MORA/ MSA

##### 1.2 Pilot Navigation SID
- contains an initial set of  instructions to all aircraft, and might show **transition routes** to navigate to appropriate fix.
- Departure routes are **solid black line**; transition routes are depicted in **dashed course line**

##### 1.3 RNAV SID
- Area Navigation (RNAV) provides guidance for operation.
- Requirements: **Two DME units, inertial reference unit (IRU), RNAV 1 standards**
- RNAV 1 standard: total system error of no more than 1 nm for 95% of the total flight time.


#### 2. Obstacle Departure Procedures (ODPs)
- Created when higher than standard takeoff minimums or climb gradients are required.
- **You may fly ODP without ATC clearance.**

#### 3. Diverse Vector Area (DVAs)
- ATC may assume responsibility for obstacle clearance. *TL;DR, flight above X within X Area*
- **DVA does not follow a specific route, but within a specified area.**




## ⭐️ Enroute
### Enroute Charts
- Airways below 18,000 feet MSL are depicted on Low Altitude Enroute Charts and are called **Victor Airways.**
- Airways at or above 18,000 feet MSL and up to FL450 are shown on High Altitude Enroute Charts and are called **Jet Routes.**
- Victor Airways - Airway extends more than **51 nm from each navaid** includes the airspace between lines **diverging at angles of 4.5 degrees** from the center of each navaid.
- If use **GPS** for navigation, you might be using **RNAV routes.**
	- Tango Routes (with letter T) are established around or through busy terminal areas.
	- Waypoints (star symbol)
	- MEA might be lower for GPS/WAAS, designated with letter G
- Airports
	- Military airports are round
	- **Airports in BLUE have instrument approach**; brown(FAA)/Green(Jepp) have no instrument approach.
- ==KEY TERMINOLOGY==: MEA, MOCA, MRA, MCA, MAA, MORA


## ⭐️Approach
### Types of Approach Procedures
#### Precision Approach (PA)
- PA provides lateral guidance to align the airplane with the runway and vertical guidance in the form of a glide slope indication.
- **ILS**
#### Approach with Vertical Guidance (APV)
- Provides lateral and vertical guidance, but not as accurate.*Basiacally non-precision PA*
- **RNAV(GPS)**
#### Non-Precision Approach (NPA)
- **Only lateral guidance** to align with runway.
- VOR localizer, NDB, RNVA minimums.
### Approach Segments
#### 0. Enroute Transition (ENR ~ IAF; Not approach yet)
##### 0.1 Standard Terminal Arrival Route (STAR)
- Published for busy airports.
- navigation from enroute **to approach fix.**

##### 0.2 Terminal Arrival Area
- RNAV(GPS) to navigate directly to IAF
- airspace with a radius of 30 nm within which you navigate direct to an IAF

##### 0.3 Feeder Route
- Shown on instrument approach charts to provide a link between an enroute fix or navaid and the IAF
- Might have several feeder routes. Use the closest one.

##### 0.4 Radar Vector
- ATC provides radar vectors to IAF


#### 1. Initial Approach (IAF ~ IF)
- ==Align airplane with approach course==
- Arc procedure, course reversal, or a straight route that intersects the final approach course.
- Course reversal is to decent the airplane to appropriate altitude for approach.

#### 2. Intermediate Approach Segment (IF ~ FAF)
- ==Airplane has been aligned center. Prepare for the final descent ==
- Normally aligned within 30° of the final approach course. (IF ~ FAF)
- If IF is not shown on the chart, the segment begins at a point where you're proceeding inbound to the FAF.
- An approach does not have a charted IAF is where your could reverse course to the runway. The intermediate segment begins when you intercept after completing the course reversal.


#### 3. Final Approach Segment (FAF ~ MAP)
- ==If have the required visual reference in sight, you can continue the approach to a landing.==
- If you cannot meet the requirement you must execute the missed approach procedure.
- Minimum descent requirements (*starts with H → height, AGL*)
	- PA/APV: Decision Altitude (DA) - MSL / Height Above Touchdown (**H**AT) - AGL
	- Non PA: Minimum Decent Altitude (MDA) - MSL
		- Straight in: Height Above Touchdown (**H**AT) - AGL
		- Circling: Height Above Airport elevation (**H**AA) - AGL


#### 4. Missed Approach Segment (MAP ~)
- Navigate from missed approach point (MAP) to where you may attempt another approach or holding pattern
- Holding pattern normally starts with DA(PA) or MAP(NPA), and ends at an IAF or Enroute fix.
- For PA, segment begins at DA
- For NPA, segment begins at MAP



## Terminology
| Term                                     | Description & Requirements                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| :--------------------------------------- | :---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| ⭐️MEA                                    | - *obstruction; signal*<br> - **Minimum Enroute Altitude** generally guarantees both **obstruction clearance** and **navigation signal coverage**, and is normally the lowest altitude you use on an airway.                                                                                                                                                                              |
| ⭐️MOCA                                   | - *sp condition; 22nm VOR; not guaranteed*<br> - **Minimum Obstruction Clearance Altitude** may be **assigned by ATC** when certain special conditions exist, and when within **22 nm** of a VOR. A MOCA does not guarantee you will receive a reliable navigation signal if you're more than 22 nm from the facility. **Identifiable by the letter T.**                                  |
| ⭐️MRA                                    | - *navaid; intersection*<br> - **Minimum Authorized Altitude** is designated where a minimum altitude is needed to **receive a navaid** away from the airway being flown in order to identify an **intersection**.                                                                                                                                                                        |
| ⭐️MCA                                    | - *low2high; climb prior*<br> - **Minimum Crossing Altitude** is the lowest altitude which an aircraft can cross when proceeding in direction of higher minimum enroute altitude (MEA). You must **begin climbing prior to reaching the fix** in order to arrive over the fix at the MCA.                                                                                                 |
| ⭐️MAA                                    | - *line-of-sight; VOR*<br> - **Maximum Authorized Altitude** is the highest flight altitude based on the **line-of-sight** transmitting distance of VOR stations using the same VOR frequency. It guarantees receiving only one signal at a time on a given frequency.                                                                                                                    |
| ⭐️Grid MORA (OROCA)                      | - *Jeppesen; 1k; m2k*<br> - **Grid Minimum Off Route Altitude** is an altitude derived by Jeppesen that provides terrain and man-made structure clearance within the grid outline by latitude and longitude lines. **In non-mountain areas, the clearance is 1,000 ft. In mountainous areas, the clearance is 2,000 ft.**                                                                 |
| Minimum Sector Altitude (MSA)            | - *Jeppeson; 1k25*<br> - The minimum safe/sector altitude (MSA), shown on Jeppesen approach charts, provides **1,000 feet of obstruction clearance within 25 nautical miles** of the indicated facility, unless some other distance is specified.                                                                                                                                         |
| Straight in Approach                     | An approach executed without performing the published course reversal.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| Course Reversal                          | - *180*<br> - Course reversal is the course to **turn the aircraft around by 180 degrees** therefore lining the aircraft up with the runway.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| Straight in Landing                      | - *30*<br> - Straight in landing minimums normally are used when final approach course is positioned within 30 degree of the runway, and a minimum of maneuvering is required to align the airplane with the runway.                                                                                                                                                                      |
| Circle to Landing                        | If final approach is not properly aligned, or if it is desirable to landing on a different runway, circling approach may be executed and circling approach minimums apply.                                                                                                                                                                                                                |
| Touchdown Zone Elevation (TDZE)          | - *3000*<br> - The highest point in the first 3,000 feet of the runway landing surface from MSL.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Airport/field Elevation                  | - *usable; ft*<br> - The highest point of an airport's usable runways measured in feet from MSL.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          |
| Threshold Crossing Height (TCH)          | - *AGL; continue*<br> - The **AGL** height at which you cross the threshold if you continue the approach to a landing while remaining on the glide slope/path.                                                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| Standard Instrument Departure (SID)      | - A standard instrument departure (SID) is depicted in graphic form and is identified by an abbreviated name and numeral, then a dot followed by the name of the exit or transition fix.<br> - If you do not have the chart for the procedure with you, do not accept a SID clearance. If you do not want to use a SID, indicate "**NO SID**" in the remarks section of your flight plan. |
| Departure End of Runway (DER)            | The physical end of the runway opposite the landing threshold used for an aircraft's takeoff.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| Air Route Traffic Control Center (ARTCC) | A facility established to provide air traffic control service to aircraft operating on instrument flight rules (IFR) flight plans within controlled airspace.                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| Terminal Instrument Procedurres (TERPs)  | Standards for a specific clearance from obstacle at a given distance from the runway based on an aircraft’s climbing at least **200 feet per nautical mile.**                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
