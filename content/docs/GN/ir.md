- ***MEA*** *obstruction; signal*
	- Minimum Enroute Altitude generally guarantees both obstruction clearance and navigation signal coverage, and is normally the lowest altitude you use on an airway.
- ***MOCA*** *sp condition; 22nm VOR; not guaranteed*
	- Minimum Obstruction Clearance Altitude. **ATC** may assign MOCA when certain special conditions exist, and when within **22 nm**  of a VOR. A MOCA does not guarantee you will receive a reliable navigation signal if you're more than 22 nm from the facility.
- ***MRA*** *navaid; intersection*
	- Minimum Reception Altitude. MRA is designated where a minimum altitude is needed to receive a navaid away from the airway being flown in order to identify an **intersection**.
- ***MCA*** *low2high; prior climb*
	- Minimum Crossing Altitude is the lowest altitude which an aircraft can cross when proceeding in direction of higher minimum enroute altitude (MEA).You must begin climbing prior to reaching the fix in order to arrive over the fix at the MCA.
- ***MAA*** *line-of-sight; VOR*
	- Maximum Authorized Altitude is the highest flight altitude based on the **line-of-sight** transmitting distance of VOR stations using the same VOR frequency. It guarantees receiving only one signal at a time on a given frequency.
- ***Grid MORA*** *Jeppesen, 1k2k*
	- Grid Minimum Off Route Altitude is an altitude derived by Jeppesen that provides terrain and man-made structure clearance within the grid outline by latitude and longitude lines. In non-mountain areas, the clearance is 1,000 ft. In mountainous areas, the clearance is 2,000 ft.
- ***Straight in Approach****
	- When pilot executes a straight in approach, the published course reversal should not be performed.
- ***Course Reversal***
	- Course reversal is the course to **turn the aircraft around by 180 degrees** therefore lining the aircraft up with the runway.
- ***Straight in Landing*** *30°*
	- Straight in landing minimums normally are used when final approach course is positioned within 30 degree of the runway, and a minimum of maneuvering is required to align the airplane with the runway.
