## Air Movement
| Movement        | So-called                                                |
|:--------------- |:-------------------------------------------------------- |
| Upward          | Convection, rising, ascending, lifting, *updrafts(fast)* |
| Downward        | **Subsidence**, sinking, descending                      | 
| Horizontal      | Advection, wind, transport                               |
| Inward **(L)**  | Convergence                                              |
| Outward **(H)** | Divergence                                               |

|:------------------------------- |:--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |:----------------------------------------------- |:------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |:----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Radiation Fog (Ground fog)      | Formed due to shallow moist layer, radiation cooling                                                                                    | Happens during nighttime and relatively shallow | Ground fog dissipate after sunrise (burn off)                                                                                              | <ul><li>Shallow moist layer</li><li>Clear skies (better radiation cooling)</li><li>Light surface wind</li></ul>                     |
| Mountain Fog                    | Happens when dense, cooler air on mountaintop sinks into valleys, and begins to fill from the bottom with cold layer of air             | -                                               | Fills valleys from the bottom with cold air                                                                                                | -                                                                                                                                   |
| Advection Fog                   | Happens when moist air moves over a colder surface                                                                                      | Can move in regardless of time                  | <ul><li>Extensive</li><li>Persistent</li><li>Move in rapidly regardless of time</li><li>Might worsen at night with radiation fog</li></ul> | <ul><li>At sea, it's called sea fog.</li><li>As wind increases, advection fog deepens.</li></ul>                                    |
| Frontal Fog (Precipitation fog) | Warm, moist air lifted over a front, clouds, and precipitation form                                                                     | -                                               | May develop from precipitation falling into cooler air                                                                                     | <ul><li>Also called frontal fog (precipitation fog).</li><li>Warm front is more likely to produce frontal fog.</li></ul>            |
| Steam Fog                       | When cold air moves over warm air, moisture evaporate from the water surface                                                            | -                                               | Often very shallow                                                                                                                         | <ul><li>Associated with shallow layer of unstable air.</li><li>Pilots can expect convective turbulence flying through it.</li></ul> |

| *Mist*                          | Intermediate fog/mist                                                                                                                   | -                                               | -                                                                                                                                          | Reported when visibility is at least **1,000m but not more than 5,000m**                                                            |

## Atmospheric Stability & Lapse Rates
| Term / Stability Type          | Definition / Value                                                                            | Weather Characteristics & Remarks                                                                                                                                                                              |
|:------------------------------ |:--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Environmental Lapse Rate (ELR) | **6°C / km** (2°C / 1,000 ft) in ISA                                                          | Degree of temperature change per distance.                                                                                                                                                                     |
| Dry Air Lapse Rate (DALR)      | **3°C per 1,000 ft** *(10°C per 1km)*                                                         | Also called Unsaturated Air Lapse Rate (UALR).                                                                                                                                                                 |
| Moist Air Lapse Rate (MALR)    | **2°C per 1,000 ft**                                                                          | Also called Saturated Air Lapse Rate (SALR). *Lower for very warm saturated parcels(1.2°C), and higher for very cold saturated parcels(3°C).*                                                                  |
| Absolute Stability             | ELR is **less than** MALR                                                                     | **Includes both isothermal and inversion temperature profiles**                                                                                                                                                |
| Absolute Instability           | ELR is **greater than** DALR                                                                  | -                                                                                                                                                                                                              | 
| Neutral Stability              | ELR = DALR or ELR = WALR(if saturated)                                                        | The column of air in the atmosphere in which ascending/descending air parcel has the same temperature/density as the enviornment.                                                                              |
| Conditional **In**stability    | An initially stable parcel at **LFC (Level of free convection)** above LCL would be unstable. | The LFC is a defining feature of a conditionally unstable column of air. **LFC will always be greater than or equal to LCL.**                                                                                  |
| Inversion (Highly Stable)      | ELR **< 0** (Temperature increases with altitude)                                             | <ul><li>Implies stable air.</li><li>Ground inversion develops due to **radiation cooling** over land, clear nights, light wind.</li><li>Inversion aloft is caused by **subsidence** (High pressure).</li></ul> |


## Stability and Corresponding Weather
| Category                   | Stable Air                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                  | Unstable Air                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
|:-------------------------- |:------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |:--------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Daily Weather**          | <ul><li>Forced aloft, widespread.</li><li>Little vertical thickness.</li><li>Precipitation will be light to moderate.</li></ul>                                                                                                                                                             | <ul><li>Goes with towering clouds, heavy precipitation.</li><li>Instability on hot summer afternoons when solar heating is intense (ELR++).</li></ul>                                                                                                                                                                             |
| **Flight Characteristics** | <ul><li>Poor low level visibility, fog may occur.</li><li>Stratus type cloud (layered clouds).</li><li>Steady precipitation.</li><li>Steady winds which can change markedly with height.</li><li>Smooth flying conditions.</li></ul>                                                        | <ul><li>Good visibility (except in precipitation).</li><li>Heap type cloud (cumulus or cumulonimbus).</li><li>Showery precipitation.</li><li>Gusty winds.</li><li>Turbulence may be moderate to severe.</li></ul>                                                                                                                 |
| **Situations**              | <ul><li>Stratiform (layer) clouds</li><li>Steady rain/drizzle</li><li>**Radiation Cooling during night near ground**</li><li>Warm air flows over cold ground</li><li>Cooling at the bottom of the troposphere</li><li>Warming in the higher troposphere</li><li>Subsiding in a higher pressure system</li></ul> | <ul><li>Cumuliform (heap) clouds</li><li>Showers/thunderstorm</li><li>**Radiation Heating during noon near ground**</li><li>**Radiation cooling at cloud top**</li><li>Cold air flows over warm ground</li><li>Warming at the bottom of the troposphere</li><li>Cooling in the higher troposphere</li><li>Lifting in a low pressure system</li></ul> | 


## (In)Stability enhancement
| Stability increases                                                                       | Instability increases                                                                                                  |
|:----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |:---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Air mass traverses a cold surface (fog)                                                   | Air mass traverses a warm surface                                                                                      |
| A column of air in the atmosphere will become more stable when it descends (**subsides**) | Air moves upward. Called **convective instability**                                                                        |
| **Nighttime radiation cooling of the surface**                                            | <ul><li>**Daytime radiation heating of the surface**</li><li>**Nighttime radiation cooling from cloud tops**</li></ul> |
- *Most of these changes occurs as a reult of the **movement of air**, but **diurnal temperature variations** can play a significant role.*


## Sources of Vertical Motion
| Source of Vertical Motion | Description & Remarks                                                                                                                                                      |
|:------------------------- |:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Orographic effects        | Air is mechanically forced upward when it encounters a terrain barrier.                                                                                                    |
| Frictional effects        | Surface wind spiral clockwise and outward from high pressure **(Convergence)**;<br>Counterclockwise and inward into low pressure **(Divergence)** due to frictional force. |
| Frontal lift              | Warmer, less dense air is forced upward over a denser, colder air mass along a frontal boundary.                                                                           |
| Buoyancy *(proactive)*    | Localized heating causes warm air to become less dense than surrounding air, making it buoyant and rise (convection).                                                      |
- ***Foehn wind**  occurs in the **Lee** of a mountain range as a consequence of the **different adiabatic** rates of moist and dry air. Foehn wind improves in the condition of lower ceiling, higher mountain, smaller WALR, or lower spread.*



## Precipitation
| Precipitation Type | Temperature Profile | Remarks |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Snow (SN) | Temperature **remains below freezing** throughout the entire depth of the atmosphere. | Intensity is based on **visibility**. |
| Ice Pellets (PL) | **Shallow** warm layer aloft, **DEEP** cold layer at the surface. | <ul><li>Occur when **inversions** are present.</li><li>Mostly happen in cold days.</li><li>Intensity is based on rate of fall.</li></ul> |
| Freezing Rain (FZRA) | **DEEP** warm layer aloft, **shallow** cold layer at the surface. | <ul><li>**Freeze** on contact with ground objects.</li><li>All "freezing" prefixed weather implies **supercooled water**.</li></ul> |
| Hail (GR)<br>**Snow Pellets(GS)**         | -                                                                                     | Produced by severe thunderstorms with strong updrafts and sufficient **supercooled water**. GR >5mm. |
- *Precipitation can be liquid or solid.*
- *Requirements: sufficient water vapor, lift, **growth process to grow large enough to fall**/*

## Growth Process
| Growth Process                                | Temperature | Mechanism & Remarks                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                           |
|:--------------------------------------------- |:----------- |:------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Warm Rain Process** <br>(Collision-Coalescence) | > 0° C      | Cloud droplets collide and merge together, growing heavy enough to fall as liquid precipitation.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| **Ice Crystal Process**                       | < 0° C      | <ul><li>**Saturation(equilibrium) vapor pressure** over water is greater than ice at the same temperature.</li><li>Because of this, water vapor **evaporates** and ice **deposits**.</li><li>Primary growth process for clouds in mid-high altitudes and colder clouds.</li><li>Supercooled water will crystallize in the presence of **freezing nuclei**.</li><li>It is very rare to find supercooled water droplets below -20°C unless there is **strong convective movement** of air (e.g., typhoon).</li></ul> |
