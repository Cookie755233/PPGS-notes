## Basics

{{< details title="One nautical mile converts to ____ km or ____ s.m. or ____ feet." closed="true" >}}
One nautical mile (NM) converts to:\
= 1.852 km (exact)\
= 1.15 mile (statute)\
= 6,000 feet
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="What does the \"vacuum\" means in the attitude indicator at the bottom? And do you adjust it?" closed="true" >}}
Vacuum indicates the attitude indicator is vacuum powered. Pilot can PULL the knob when starting the engine to spin up the gyro, and turn the knob to adjust the pitch/bank to the correct horizon.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="Standard rate of turn?" closed="true" >}}
3 degrees per second or 360 degree turn in 2 minutes.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="Slipping turn? Skidding turn?" closed="true" >}}
A ball called the "inclinometer" or "slip/skid indicator" shows the quality of the turn — whether "coordinated."\
\
In a **SLIP**, the rate of turn is too slow for the angle of bank, and the ball moves to the INSIDE of the turn.\
\
In a **SKID**, the rate of turn is too great for the angle of bank, and the ball moves to the OUTSIDE of the turn.
{{< /details >}}

Left

{{< details title="What triggers 'PITOT FAIL' and 'PITOT OFF' means when displayed on the PFD?" closed="true" >}}

Pitot (heater) OFF if:\

{{< details title="What triggers the DOOR OPEN warning?" closed="true" >}}
Canopy and/or rear door is not closed AND locked.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="What triggers OIL PRES. LO? and what does it imply?" closed="true" >}}
Oil pressure is below 25 psi.\
When oil pressure is below 25 psi, it implies that the ENGINE OIL PIPE could be broken/leaking, thus the temperature of the engine could raise due to high friction. It is suggested to land ASAP.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="What triggers ALTERNATOR? and what does it imply?" closed="true" >}}
Alternator failed. Battery is the only electronic source.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="What triggers STARTER ENGD? and what does it imply?" closed="true" >}}
Starter is irregularly engaged and could cause a burn or gear damage.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="When will the crosshatch flag appear? On which instrument?" closed="true" >}}
A crosshatched area appears on some altimeters when displaying an altitude below 10,000 feet or 3,000 MSL. It indicates a critical phase of flight including taxiing, takeoffs and etc.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="What are the two standard lapse rates?" closed="true" >}}
The standard pressure lapse rate is approximately 1 inch of mercury per thousand feet of altitude.\
The standard temperature lapse rate approximates 2°C per thousand feet of altitude.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="QNH is set to 1013 mbar altimeter shows 500 feet airport elevation. If pressure changes to 1023 mbar what will the altimeter indicate?" closed="true" >}}
200 ft\
\
—\
An aircraft altimeter is a barometer that measures the ambient atmospheric pressure and translates it into altitude based on the pressure setting you dial into it. In the lower atmosphere, pressure increases/drops by roughly 1 mbar for every 30 feet of altitude gained/descended.\
\
Because the actual atmospheric pressure has increased by 10 mbar, the air at the airport is now denser and higher in pressure.\
\
Since the altimeter is still looking for the lower 1013 mbar pressure level to register its baseline, it senses this higher pressure as being "closer to sea level" or lower down in the atmosphere. It will therefore read lower by approximately 300 feet (\(10 \times 30\)) dropping from its initial 500 feet reading down to 200 feet.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="Transition level vs altitude" closed="true" >}}
**Transition Level**: (High \(\rightarrow\) Low)\
This is the lowest Flight Level available for cruise flight above the transition altitude. When descending down through this level, pilots change their altimeter back to the local QNH setting.\
\
**Transition Altitude**: (Low \(\rightarrow\) High)\
This is the highest altitude where you still fly using the local atmospheric pressure (QNH). When climbing up through this altitude, pilots change their altimeter setting to the standard 1013 mbar (QNE).
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="!Absolute altitude?" closed="true" >}}
Absolute altitude is the actual height above the EARTH's surface.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="Density altitude?" closed="true" >}}
Pressure altitude corrected for non-standard atmospheric temperature used in engine power calculations.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="Whats Altimeter trend vector?" closed="true" >}}
It shows where the aircraft's altitude will be in the next 6 seconds.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="Under what scenarios would cause a slightly greater altitude" closed="true" >}}
1. Opening the alternate static source (after static blockage), due to the venturi effect when air coming from the air inlet located in the front of the nose of the aircraft. Air speed increases, while pressure decreases.\
\
2. Leaking pressure line (blue/purple line) causing a lower pressure input sent to the altimeter.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="What would you do if there's no alternate static source?" closed="true" >}}
Break the VSI glass.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="in DA40, what do you do when there's a static blockage?" closed="true" >}}
There's an alternate static source on the left fuselage. Pilots can OPEN the alternate static, but make sure the EMERGENCY WINDOW and COCKPIT VENT are CLOSED.
{{< /details >}}

1 knot nearly equals to a minute of LATITUDE\
= 1 NM/hour = 1.852 km/hour = 1.15 MPH
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="Tolerance on account of instrument error is?" closed="true" >}}

{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="When static blocks, VSI = ?" closed="true" >}}
When static blocks, the wafer pressure = case pressure, hence VSI = 0
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="What will happen when turn on alternate static source?" closed="true" >}}
1. Altimeter indicates a slightly higher altitude than actual.\
2. ASI indicates an airspeed greater than the actual.\
3. VSI shows a momentary climb.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="What's the main difference between altimeter and VSI?" closed="true" >}}
Static port air is directly vented into the ALTIMETER CASE, while air enters the WAFER IN VSI.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="When will Equivalent airspeed starts to pronounce?" closed="true" >}}
Compressibility error happens when \(>200\text{ kts}\) or \(>20,000\text{ ft}\).
{{< /details >}}
