## G1000 System
The G1000 currently has 3 ways of ensuring that its data is correct: **(IFR)**
1. **Internal system monitoring**: Internal monitoring uses built-in algorithm to ensure that displayed data is consistent and fault-free.
2. **Fault Detection & Exclusion (FDE)** - Assesses any anomalies in the satellite system and removes the “bad" satellite from the data equation, providing a seamless, correct data stream.
3. **Receiver Autonomous Integrity Monitoring (RAIM)** - FDE RAIM requires a minimum (**five** satellites to be in ‘sight' of the GPS unit in order for it to obtain enough verified information for a GPS approach

## System Failures
| Scenarios                       | Key Characteristics & Behavior                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 |
|:------------------------------- |:-------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Essential Bus**               | - Switch location: Lower left corner, **provides 30 minutes battery if alternator fails to shed load from system.** <br> - Only allows essential components to show *( NAV/COM 1, transponder, standby/attitude gyro, engine instruments(PFD), annunciator panel, GPS, landing light, pitot heat, and flaps.)* <br> - Shuts off Non-essential avionics, autopilot (in some configurations), NAV/COM 2, and other secondary cabin electronics.                  |
| **Complete System Failure**     | - Essential bus (main battery) may run for **30 minute**. <br> - Essential power (lithium power battery) runs **standby attitude indicator** and one **flood light** for 90 minutes. However, no indication of yaw. *(last straw)*                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| **Stuck Microphone**            | - If the push-to-talk (PTT) Key becomes stuck, the COM transmitter stops transmitting after **35 seconds** of continuous operation. <br> - An alert appears on the PFD to advise the crew of a stuck microphone. <br> - The COM1 MIC or COM2 MIC Key Annunciator on the Audio Panel continues to flash as long as the PTT Key remains stuck.                                                                                                                   |
| **COM Tuning Failure**          | - In case of a COM system tuning failure, the emergency frequency (121.500 **MHz**) is automatically tuned in the radio in which the tuning failure occurred. <br> - *press and hold for 2 second on the freq transfer key to quickly set 121.5*                                                                                                                                                                                                               |
| **CO Detector Alert / Failure** | - Located in the top right corner next to ELT. <br> - Self test begins when power is applied, flashing *twice*, and remains off until another CO alert. <br> - Stays on until the CO level goes below **50 ppm.** <br> - **Failure(CO/temperature sensor) indication: Flash every 4 seconds**<br><hr style="margin: 5px">**Procedure** <br> 1. Press TEST/RESET button to confirm, if continues - <br> 2. Cabin heat - OFF; Ventilation - OPEN; Emergency Window - OPEN; Canopy - OPEN. |
| **Fail Safe Operation**         | If there is a failure of the Audio Panel, a **fail-safe circuit connects the pilots headset and microphone directly to the COM 1 transceiver**, Audio is not available on the speaker.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| **Tuning Failure**              | In case of a COM system tuning failure, the emergency frequency (121.500 MHz) is automatically tuned in the radio in which the tuning failure occurred.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                        |


### Line Replacement Unit (LRU)  Failures
| Failed Component | System Functions & Key Data                                                                                        |
|:---------------- |:------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |

| **GDC74A**       | Loss OAT, TAS, ASI, ALT and VSI on PFD, transponder loses mode C capabilities.                                     |
| **GRS77**        | Attitude presentations are removed from PFD and replaced with "X" and attitude, heading data is lost.              |
| **GMU44**        | Stabilized heading data is lost.                                                                                   |
| **OAT sensor**   | Red "X" appears on TAS and OAT                                                                                     |
| **GTX345**       | Red "X" appears over transponder box on PFD and advisory message appears.                                          |
| **GEA 71**       | ALL engine/airframe data is lost.                                                                                  |

### Wave Transportation
![](/images/skip.png "")

| Wave Type     | Key Characteristics & Behavior                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| :------------ | :------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Ground Wave   | - Radio wave traveling along the earth’s surface. <br> - Depends on: Power, frequency, electrical conductivity on the surface *(water > dry surface)*. <br> - Higher frequency = shorter ground range.                                                                                                                                                                                            |
| Sky Wave      | - Radio wave reflected from the **ionosphere**. <br> - Higher frequency wave may penetrate the ionosphere. <br> - Smaller angle, likely to be a total reflection. <br> - **LF/MF would be absorbed by the D-layer during daytime.** {{< tip "D-layer disappear at night" >}}  <br> - **HF allows worldwide coverage regardless of time.** <br> - **UHF travels at line of sight (NOT sky wave).** |
| Skip Distance | **Skip Distance** is the distance from the transmitting station to the point on the surface of the earth at which the first sky wave can be received.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             |
| Skip Zone     | **Skip zone** is an area on the surface of the earth where no reception of any radio waves is possible. (*End of ground wave ~ start of sky wave*)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                |

## ELT
{{< callout >}}
**Emergency Locator Transmitter ELT**
- located in the rear fuselage, below the aft baggage compartment.
- ELT antenna is on a bracket in the aft fuselage, above the ELT.
- Frequency: **121.5 MHz(50hr) and 406 MHz(24hr)** 
- Activation: **Manual switch**, **Sea water**, **Acceleration switch more than**
- Purpose: locate survivors for **SAR** (Search and Rescue)
- Information sent: **Country of registration, Aircraft Registration,  Position coordinates, Serial number** *CAPS*

## Remarks
- Cabin check: **Terrain, Obstacles, Navigation, Airport Directory (TONA)**
- Switch tanks at intervals no greater than 30 minutes. When switching, the **electrical fuel pump shall switch ON** 
- 7 Checklists: **Check before/after engine start; before takeoff check; climb to cruise check; descent/approach check; after landing check; parking check.**
- Red extreme pitch warning *chevrons* would show when **pitch +50 or -30**
- Sterile cockpit rules (FAR 121.542): During critical phases of flight, including **all ground operations such as taxiing, takeoff, and landing, and all flight operations below 10,000 ft**, non-essential activities are not allowed.
- At level off, switch tanks. After initial tank switch, switch tanks at intervals no greater than 30 minutes. Maximum differential quantity between left and right tanks is 10 US gallons. **While switching from one tank to the other, the electrical fuel pump shall be switched ON.**
