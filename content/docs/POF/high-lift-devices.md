---
title: 3. High Lift Devices
---

{{< details title="What are flap used for" closed="true" >}}
Flaps are used in low speed operation to generate enough lift to prevent the aircraft from stalling.

When flaps is used (down), the stalling angle is LOWER, the center of lift/pressure moves AFT.

\(L = \frac{1}{2} \times \rho \times V^2 \times C_L\)
When \(V\) is smaller, we must increase \(C_L\) to create the enough lift.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="Critical AoA with flaps vs Critical AoA without flaps" closed="true" >}}
Critical AoA with flaps is LOWER than Critical AoA without flaps.

\(C_{L,max}\) with flaps is LESS than \(C_{L,max}\) without flaps.

**C-AoA with flaps down is lowered but require less AoA to generate the same lift.**
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="Types of flap" closed="true" >}}
1. Plain flap is simple flap attached to the wing by a hinge to increase lift.

2. Split flap is hinged to the lower portion of the wing to increase the lift but causes MORE FORM DRAG than plain flag.

3. Slotted flap increases its camber and allow higher energy wind passing through slots to delay boundary layer separation on the upper surface.

4. Fowler flap increases camber and wing's surface to allow lower stall speeds.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="Leading edge flaps vs Trailing edge flaps" closed="true" >}}
Leading edge flaps increases critical AoA so that the aircraft can be flown at higher AoA, which increases the lift, without stalling. (extends the \(C_L\) - AoA slope)

Trailing edge flaps, however, would decrease the critical AoA, but increase the coefficient of lift, which generates more lift at a lower AoA. (Shift the slope upward)
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="Takeoff flaps vs landing flaps" closed="true" >}}
Beyond half-flap position, extended flap provides more increase in drag than increase in lift. 

TAKEOFF FLAP
- Takeoff flap is limited to less than half flaps.
- shorten the takeoff roll due to slower lift-off speed.
- climb performance is reduced.

LANDING FLAP
- Landing flap is usually in full down position.
- lower approach speed due to higher \(C_L\), provides a steeper descent angle.
- reduces the landing roll due to slower landing speed.
{{< /details >}}

{{< details title="Dirty configuration → Clean configuration, what changes?" closed="true" >}}
1. LIFT
Flaps up \(\rightarrow\) \(C_L\) reduces \(\rightarrow\) increase AoA to maintain enough lift \(\rightarrow\) nose up

2. DRAG
Flaps up \(\rightarrow\) \(C_D\) reduces \(\rightarrow\) improves the accleration

3. MOMENT
Flaps up \(\rightarrow\) Center of Lift FORWARD \(\rightarrow\) Pitch down moment DECREASED \(\rightarrow\) A slight increase in nose up tendency \(\rightarrow\) may require re-trimming.
However, the nose up moment is compensated by the decrease of \(C_L\), pilot would still have to pull onto the control handle to maintain a level flight.
{{< /details >}}
