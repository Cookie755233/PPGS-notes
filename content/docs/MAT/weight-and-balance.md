## Weight Definitions
![](/images/weightdef.png "")

- Empty weight (dry): Weight or airframe, power plant, and equipment before any oil or fuel added.
- Empty weight (wet): Empty weight (dry) + Unusable fuel/oil + Hydraulic fluid
- **Basic Empty Weight (BEW): The empty weight (wet) plus weights of engine oil.**
- Zero fuel weight: BEW plus payload (weights of passengers, baggages and pilots)
- Takeoff weight: Zero fuel weight plus takeoff fuel (TOF)
- Ramp weight: Takeoff weight plus fuel weights on start, taxiing and run up.
- Landing weight: Takeoff weight minus trip fuel.
- Block fuel: Takeoff fuel(reserve fuel + trip fuel) + Taxi fuel

## Weight Limits
- Aircraft is limited to:
	- Maximum **Tank** Capacity is considered in **long** haul flights
		- Require lots, or full tanks
	- Maximum **Traffic** Load is considered in **short** haul flights
		- Can take up more payload
		- Zero Fuel Weight is concerned


## CG
- The center of gravity (C.G.) is an imaginary point at which all the aircraft weights concentrated.
- CG position would affect *Longitudinal Stability (Pitch)*
